Welcome
to the Lourve

Home to Leonardo da Vinci's Mona Lisa, the Lourve is considered the word's greatest art museum, with an unparalleled collection of items covering the full spectrum of art through the ages.
The Lourve hardly needs an introduction -- it is still the most visited museum in the world, with over 10 million visitors each year.

The Palace

Explore the former palace of the kings of France - now the largest museum in the world covering almost 10,000 years of history! The palace is divided into three wings: Richelieu, Sully and Denon.

The Marly courtyard

Under the glass roofs of Cours Marly and Puget, the masterpieces of French sculpture find natural light! The statues brought together in the Louvre were often designed for the outdoors, in particular for the gardens of the palaces of Versailles or the Tuileries.

Hall of Caryatids

The ballroom of the kings of France marks the beginning of this new artistic style in Paris. And since the 17th century, it has housed a prestigious collection of ancient sculptures under its ample vaults.
